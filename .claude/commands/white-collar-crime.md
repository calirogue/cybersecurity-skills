## 3. THE CORRUPTION & DECEPTION MECHANISMS

### Operational Mechanics

Map the step-by-step execution of the fraud scheme. Use the appropriate phase model for the crime type:

**For Accounting/Securities Fraud — use the Fraud Triangle + Execution Chain:**
1. **Pressure** — What incentive created the motive? (earnings targets, debt covenants, executive bonuses)
2. **Opportunity** — What control gaps were exploited? (weak audit committee, inadequate segregation of duties, offshore subsidiaries)
3. **Rationalization** — How did perpetrators justify the conduct internally?
4. **Execution** — Exactly which ledger entries, disclosures, or transactions were falsified and how.
5. **Concealment** — Methods used to hide the fraud (document destruction, related-party transactions, special purpose entities).

**For Money Laundering — use the Three-Phase Model:**
1. **Placement** — Introduction of illicit cash into the financial system (structuring/smurfing, cash-intensive businesses, real estate purchases).
2. **Layering** — Obscuring the audit trail (shell companies, wire transfers across jurisdictions, cryptocurrency conversion, trade-based laundering).
3. **Integration** — Reintroduction of funds as legitimate assets (luxury goods, investment portfolios, corporate dividends).

**For Bribery/FCPA — use the Corrupt Payment Chain:**
1. Identification of foreign official and business objective.
2. Structuring payment through intermediaries (agents, consultants, joint venture partners).
3. Disguise of payments in corporate books (false invoices, sham consulting agreements).
4. Benefit received (contract award, regulatory approval, license).

**For Insider Trading — use the Information Flow Map:**
1. Source of material nonpublic information (MNPI).
2. Tipper-tippee relationship and benefit transferred.
3. Trading in securities before public disclosure.
4. Cover-up through personal accounts, offshore accounts, or nominee traders.

### Civil vs. Criminal Enforcement

**Civil Enforcement (burden: preponderance of evidence)**
- SEC disgorgement of ill-gotten gains (*Liu v. SEC*, 2020 — limited to net profits)
- Civil monetary penalties (up to 3x the gain or loss avoided under Exchange Act)
- Officer and director bars (SEC Rule 102(e))
- Cease-and-desist orders
- Monitorship and compliance undertakings (DPAs / NPAs)

**Criminal Prosecution (burden: beyond a reasonable doubt)**
- Federal indictment by grand jury
- Sentencing under USSG Chapter 2B — enhancements for loss amount, victim count, abuse of trust, sophisticated means
- Asset forfeiture (18 U.S.C. §§ 981–982)
- Prison sentences: Wire Fraud up to 20 yrs; Securities Fraud up to 25 yrs; RICO up to 20 yrs per count
- Corporate criminal liability via respondeat superior

### Key Investigative Techniques
- **Grand Jury Subpoenas** — Compel document production and testimony without probable cause.
- **Whistleblower Programs** — SEC program (Dodd-Frank § 922): 10–30% of sanctions over $1M; False Claims Act: 15–30% of government recovery.
- **Cooperation Agreements** — Assessed under the Yates Memo (individual accountability) and the FCPA Corporate Enforcement Policy.
- **Forensic Accounting** — Benford's Law analysis, journal entry testing, related-party transaction mapping.
- **Digital Forensics** — Email metadata, deleted file recovery, trading platform logs, encrypted communications.
- **MLATs** — For cross-border evidence gathering in offshore jurisdictions.
